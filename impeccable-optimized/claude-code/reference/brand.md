# Brand register

Design IS the product. Loaded by SKILL.md Setup when register = brand. Applies to: brand sites, landing pages, marketing surfaces, campaign pages, portfolios, long-form content, about pages.

## The stance

Communicate, not transact. A visitor's impression IS the thing being made. The brand register spans every genre — tech (Stripe, Linear, Vercel), luxury (hotels, fashion), consumer (food, travel, CPG), creative (studios, agencies, bands). They share **stance**; they diverge wildly in **aesthetic lane**. Don't collapse them.

## The brand slop test

If a visitor could look at this and say "AI made that" without doubt, it's failed. The bar is distinctiveness; a visitor should ask "how was this made?", not "which AI made this?"

Restraint without intent reads as mediocre, not refined. Brand surfaces need a POV, a specific audience, a willingness to risk strangeness.

## Second slop test: aesthetic lane

Before committing, name the reference. Lanes:
- Klim-style specimen page
- Stripe-minimal
- Liquid-Death-acid-maximalism
- Editorial-typographic
- Brutalist-utility
- Industrial signage
- Mid-century postwar
- Bauhaus-grid
- Y2K-nostalgic
- Vintage-product (cigarette packaging, vinyl sleeves)

Don't drift into editorial-magazine aesthetics on a brief that isn't editorial. A hiking brand with Cormorant italic drop caps has the wrong register-within-register.

## Reflex-reject aesthetic lanes (currently saturated)

Avoid these without explicit register reason:

- **Editorial-typographic.** Display serif (italic) + small mono labels + ruled separators + monochromatic restraint. Klim-influenced. By 2026, every Stripe-adjacent / Notion-adjacent brand lands here.
- **AI-dark-mode-with-purple.** Purple gradients on near-black, glowing particles, neon accents. AI tool reflex.
- **Healthcare-cream-and-teal.** Pale cream surface + soft teal accent + Inter. Wellness reflex.
- **Crypto-neon-on-black.** Saturated neon green/cyan on full black. Crypto reflex.

When the brand has already committed to a lane as identity, identity-preservation wins. Reflex-reject applies to **greenfield** decisions.

## Brand typography

### Font selection
Per `reference/typography.md` procedure. Strict reflex-reject list (Fraunces, Cormorant, Inter, Plex, etc.).

### Pairing voice by brand type
- Editorial / long-form / luxury: display serif + sans body
- Tech / dev tools / fintech: one committed sans
- Consumer / food / travel: humanist sans + script or display serif
- Industrial / utility: geometric sans + mono for technical
- Cultural / arts: one type-as-identity

## Brand color

Per `reference/color-and-contrast.md`. Brand can go Committed, Full palette, or Drenched freely. Restrained is the brand-minimalism choice — opt in, not default.

## Brand layout

Editorial composition. Asymmetry allowed. Vary rhythm aggressively. Custom layouts > grid-conformance.

## Brand motion

Signature moments allowed. Scroll-driven reveals. Hero entrance.

## Imagery

Image-led briefs (restaurants, hotels, magazines, photography, hobbyist communities, food, travel, fashion, product) need REAL or sourced imagery. Don't replace with CSS scenery, fake metrics, decorative panels, or filler copy.

## Brand-register-specific failures

- Picking a category-default palette
- Landing in a reflex-reject aesthetic lane
- Editorial-magazine treatment on a non-editorial brief
- Pure restraint without POV (reads as mediocre)
- Generic "modern" type pairing
- AI-marketing-template aesthetic
